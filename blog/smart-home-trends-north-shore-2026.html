5 SMART HOME TRENDS NORTH SHORE HOMEOWNERS SHOULD ACT ON BEFORE THE END OF 2026

March 28, 2026By Abvolt Automation8 min read

The smart home industry has crossed a tipping point. What was once a novelty reserved for early adopters is now essential infrastructure for luxury properties. Nearly 60% of U.S. households are expected to have at least one smart device by the end of this year, and the homes we work on across Lake Forest, Winnetka, and Highland Park are leading that curve. The question is no longer whether to invest in smart home technology — it's which investments deliver the most value right now.

After completing dozens of projects in the first quarter of 2026 and attending CES earlier this year, our team has identified five trends that are reshaping how North Shore homeowners think about their properties. These aren't speculative — they're upgrades we're installing today.

1. AI-Powered Automation That Actually Learns

The biggest shift we're seeing is the move from programmed scenes to genuinely adaptive automation. Platforms like Control4's X4 and Josh.ai's JoshGPT now use machine learning to study your household patterns — when you typically arrive home, your preferred lighting at different times of day, how you use climate zones — and adjust automatically without manual programming. The result is a home that anticipates your needs rather than waiting for commands. For large estates where different family members have different routines, this is transformative.

2. Circadian Lighting Goes Mainstream

Lutron's Lumaris and Ketra systems have matured considerably. Circadian lighting adjusts color temperature throughout the day — cool, energizing tones in the morning and warm, relaxing hues in the evening — to support your body's natural sleep-wake cycle. When paired with motorized shades that track the sun's position using GPS coordinates, the combined system manages natural and artificial light seamlessly. We're seeing this requested on nearly every new project, and homeowners consistently report better sleep and improved energy levels within weeks of installation.

3. The Matter Protocol Ends the Compatibility Headache

For years, one of the biggest frustrations in smart home technology was getting devices from different manufacturers to communicate reliably. The Matter protocol has changed that. With over 550 companies now producing Matter-compatible devices, homeowners can mix brands without worrying about ecosystem lock-in. Apple HomeKit, Google Home, Amazon Alexa, and Samsung SmartThings all speak the same language. For our clients, this means future upgrades are simpler, less expensive, and won't require replacing existing infrastructure.

4. Whole-Home Energy Intelligence

Energy management has become a central pillar of smart home design — and not just for environmental reasons. Smart thermostats, intelligent breaker panels, and integrated EV charger scheduling are delivering meaningful cost reductions. Homes we've outfitted with comprehensive energy monitoring and automation are seeing 20-30% reductions in utility bills. With ComEd's $2,500 EV charger rebate still available in 2026, the financial case for energy-intelligent homes has never been stronger. The integration between solar panels, battery storage, EV charging, and load management is something we're designing into every new construction project.

5. Security Powered by Contextual AI

Home security cameras with AI analytics have moved well beyond basic motion detection. Modern systems from Verkada and others can distinguish between family members, delivery drivers, vehicles, and animals — virtually eliminating false alerts. They flag unusual patterns rather than just movement, and integrate deeply with access control and automation. When the system recognizes your car pulling into the driveway, it can disarm the alarm, unlock the garage, and turn on pathway lights — all before you reach the door. For North Shore estates with multiple entry points and seasonal vacancy, this level of contextual intelligence provides peace of mind that basic alarm systems never could.

The Common Thread: Professional Integration Matters More Than Ever

Every one of these trends shares a requirement: they work best when designed and installed as part of a unified system. A circadian lighting system that doesn't coordinate with your shades is half the benefit. AI automation that can't communicate with your security is a missed opportunity. The technology has become more capable — but that means the integration work has become more important, not less. This is why CEDIA reports that professional integrators are being brought into renovation and construction projects earlier than ever before.

Frequently Asked Questions
